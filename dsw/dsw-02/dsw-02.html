What is a design token?

A design token is a named, platform-agnostic representation of a design decision — a color, a spacing value, a typeface — stored as a key-value pair that can be transformed into any output format.

What is OKLCH?

OKLCH is a perceptually uniform polar colorspace. Equal numerical distances in OKLCH correspond to equal perceived differences in color — unlike HSL or RGB, where the same numerical step produces perceptually unequal results at different points in the space.

What is APCA?

The Advanced Perceptual Contrast Algorithm is a contrast calculation method that accounts for spatial frequency, polarity, and font weight — giving more accurate predictions of readability than the WCAG 2.x relative luminance ratio.

Understanding Portfolio Rebalancing

Portfolio rebalancing is the process of realigning the weights of your portfolio's assets to maintain your desired level of asset allocation and risk. Over time, as different assets grow at different rates, your portfolio can drift from its target allocation.

When to rebalance

Most financial advisors recommend rebalancing when your portfolio deviates more than 5 percentage points from your target allocation, or on a regular calendar schedule — typically quarterly or annually.

The best rebalancing strategy is the one you will actually execute consistently.

  • Threshold-based: rebalance when allocation drifts beyond a set percentage
  • Calendar-based: rebalance on a fixed schedule regardless of drift
  • Hybrid: use calendar triggers with drift thresholds as guardrails

Consider tax implications when rebalancing a taxable account. Selling appreciated assets triggers capital gains. tax-loss harvesting can offset some of this liability.
